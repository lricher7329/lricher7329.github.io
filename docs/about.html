About

Lawrence Richer, MD, FRCPC

I am a pediatric neurologist, clinician-scientist, and Associate Dean (Research) in the College of Health Sciences at the University of Alberta. My work sits at the intersection of clinical medicine, research strategy, and systems design — building infrastructure that helps researchers and clinicians translate discoveries into better patient outcomes.

Institutional Leadership

As Associate Dean (Research), I oversee research strategy, funding development, and infrastructure across the College of Health Sciences. This includes:

  • Clinical Research Institute (CRI) — strategic direction and operational oversight for institutional clinical research
  • AI + Health Hub — co-leading the University’s initiative to embed artificial intelligence in health research
  • Kipnes Research Institute — contributing to the launch and strategic planning of this new research institute
  • Research Hubs — oversight of the Regenerative Medicine Hub, Healthy Aging Hub, and Collective Health Research Hub

Clinical Practice

I maintain an active clinical practice in pediatric neurology at the Stollery Children’s Hospital, with subspecialty expertise in:

  • Autonomic disorders — including POTS, autonomic neuropathies, and dysautonomia
  • Headache medicine — pediatric and adolescent migraine, chronic daily headache
  • Rare neurological diseases — metabolic and genetic conditions affecting the nervous system

I also provide medico-legal expert opinions for the Canadian Medical Protective Association (CMPA) in pediatric neurology.

Teaching & Mentorship

Teaching is central to my academic identity. I contribute through:

  • Hybrid Pediatric Neurology Case Rounds
  • Neuroscience Grand Rounds
  • Neuro 210 — medical school neuroscience teaching
  • Clinical Trials Workshop — methodology education for trainees and faculty
  • Direct supervision of graduate students and research trainees (see Team)
